- COMMENTS
  1. The NAT2 enzyme acts on the metabolism of various drugs, including isoniazid, and
     carcinogens, reducing or increasing their toxicity. Therefore, NAT2 genetic
     polymorphism determines individual susceptibility to drugs and carcinogens.

  2. Anti-tuberculosis drug-induced hepatotoxicity is closely related to NAT2 activity.
     In slow acetylators, the odds ratio for hepatotoxicity has been reported as 3.73
     compared with rapid or intermediate acetylators.
     (Int J Tuberc Lung Dis. 2012)
     Because intermediate acetylators also have lower-than-normal enzyme activity,
     follow-up monitoring of hepatic function and consideration of dose reduction are
     recommended.

  3. The *4, *6, and *7 allele frequencies commonly found in Koreans are 0.657, 0.201,
     and 0.115, respectively. (Clin Chem 2002)

  4. The above results were prepared using the Named Alleles data from ClinPGx.
     (https://www.clinpgx.org/gene/PA18/haplotype, accessed 2024.3.12)
