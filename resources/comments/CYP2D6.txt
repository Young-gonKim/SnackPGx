- COMMENTS
1. CYP2D6 is a metabolic enzyme involved in the metabolism of various drugs, including
   antidepressants (TCAs and SSRIs), antipsychotics, antiarrhythmics, beta-blockers,
   opioid analgesics, tamoxifen, and anticancer agents.

2. In intermediate metabolizers (IM), dose reduction may need to be considered when
   administering CYP2D6 substrate drugs in the future, and caution is advised when
   co-administering drugs that act as CYP2D6 inhibitors; monitoring of blood drug
   concentrations is recommended. Note that for tamoxifen, which is metabolized by
   CYP2D6 into its active metabolite endoxifen, reduced CYP2D6 activity conversely leads
   to decreased therapeutic effect.

   CYP2D6 substrate drugs include all TCAs, SSRIs such as fluoxetine and paroxetine,
   antipsychotics (e.g., haloperidol, risperidone), and antiarrhythmics (e.g.,
   metoprolol, carvedilol). Strong CYP2D6 inhibitors include fluoxetine, paroxetine,
   bupropion, and ritonavir.

3. This test examines only some of the variant sites included in the major variant
   alleles commonly found in Koreans (*5 and *10 alleles, and multiplication); if
   necessary, additional testing for the remaining variant alleles can be performed.

   In Koreans, the CYP2D6*10 allele is the most common (45%), and CYP2D6*5 is reported
   at a frequency of 6%.
   (Ther Drug Monit. 2006 Jun;28(3):382-7.)
