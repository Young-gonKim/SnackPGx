- COMMENTS
1. TPMT is an enzyme involved in the s-methylation of azathioprine, 6-mercaptopurine,
   and thioguanine. Enzyme activity is determined by TPMT genetic polymorphism.
   In patients carrying variant alleles that reduce TPMT metabolic capacity, drug
   toxicity such as myelosuppression is common, and a higher rate of treatment
   discontinuation has been reported. Therefore, when administering related drugs, the
   dose should be adjusted according to the TPMT genotype: dose reduction to <10% of the
   wild type for homozygotes of a TPMT variant, and to 30-70% for heterozygotes.
   (Ref. Clin Pharmacol Ther. 2019 May;105(5):1095-1105)

2. The TPMT *1, *3C, and *6 allele frequencies in Koreans are reported as 98.1%, 1.4%,
   and 0.2%, respectively.
   (Ref. Pharmacogenetics and Genomics 2015, 25:143-146)

3. The above results were prepared using the Gene-specific Information Tables from
   ClinPGx. (https://www.clinpgx.org/page/tpmtRefMaterials, downloaded 2025.10.20)

4. Note that genetic factors not identified by this assay and non-genetic factors such
   as drug-drug interactions may affect drug response. Therefore, comprehensive
   interpretation of the results and determination of the application policy are required.
