- COMMENTS
1. UGT1A1 is a Phase II enzyme involved in the metabolism of SN-38, the active
   metabolite of irinotecan; enzyme activity is determined by the UGT1A1 genotype.
   In patients carrying a genotype that reduces UGT1A1 activity, the risk of
   dose-limiting toxicities such as myelosuppression and diarrhea increases during
   irinotecan therapy.
   (Ref. Pharmgenomics Pers Med. 2017;10:61-8.)

   In particular, a 30% dose reduction has been recommended during irinotecan therapy
   for patients with a poor metabolizer genotype, whereas dose reduction is not
   recommended for intermediate metabolizers or normal metabolizers.
   (Ref. Eur J Hum Genet. 2023;31(9):982-7)

2. The *6, *27, and *28 allele frequencies in Koreans are 21.3%, 1.4%, and 12.7%,
   respectively.
   (Clin Chem. 2003;49(12):2078-81, Ann Lab Med. 2020:40(3):281-3)

3. The above results were prepared using the Gene-specific Information Tables from
   ClinPGx. (https://www.clinpgx.org/page/ugt1a1RefMaterials, downloaded 2025.10.20)

4. Note that genetic factors not identified by this assay and non-genetic factors such
   as drug-drug interactions may affect drug response. Therefore, comprehensive
   interpretation of the results and determination of the application policy are required.
