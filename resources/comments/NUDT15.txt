- COMMENTS
1. NUDT15 is an enzyme involved in the conversion of cytotoxic thioguanine triphosphate
   into non-toxic thioguanine monophosphate. Enzyme activity is determined by NUDT15
   genetic polymorphism.

2. In patients carrying variant alleles that reduce NUDT15 enzyme activity, adverse
   effects such as early-onset leukopenia have been reported to be common when using
   related drugs such as azathioprine, 6-mercaptopurine, and thioguanine. Therefore, for
   intermediate metabolizers, dose reduction to 30-80% is recommended, and for poor
   metabolizers, dosing below 10% or substitution with another drug is recommended.
   (Ref. Clin Pharmacol Ther. 2019 May;105(5):1095-1105)

3. The NUDT15 *1, *2, *3, *4, *5, and *6 allele frequencies in Koreans are reported as
   86.7%, 4.4%, 6.9%, 0.4%, 1.1%, and 0.5%, respectively.
   (Ref. Pharmacogenet Genomics. 2017 May;27(5):197-200)

4. The above results were prepared using the Gene-specific Information Tables from
   ClinPGx. (https://www.clinpgx.org/page/nudt15RefMaterials, downloaded 2025.10.20)

5. Note that genetic factors not identified by this assay and non-genetic factors such
   as drug-drug interactions may affect drug response. Therefore, comprehensive
   interpretation of the results and determination of the application policy are required.
