- COMMENTS
  1. CYP2C9 is a hepatic enzyme involved in the metabolism of drugs such as warfarin,
     phenytoin, and sulfonylureas. Enzyme activity is determined by the CYP2C9 genotype.

  2. The CYP2C9 *1 and *3 allele frequencies in Koreans have been reported as 0.934 and
     0.060, respectively. (* Ref. Br J Clin Pharmacol 2005;60:418-22)

  3. The above results were prepared using the Gene-specific Information Tables from
     ClinPGx. (https://www.clinpgx.org/page/cyp2c9RefMaterials, downloaded 2025.10.20)

  4. Note that genetic factors not identified by this assay and non-genetic factors such
     as drug-drug interactions may affect drug response. Therefore, comprehensive
     interpretation of the results and determination of the application policy are
     required.
