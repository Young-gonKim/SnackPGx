- COMMENTS
  1. MTHFR is a key enzyme involved in intracellular folate homeostasis/metabolism,
     converting 5,10-methylenetetrahydrofolate into 5-methyltetrahydrofolate. When the
     MTHFR c.665C>T (legacy name C677T) polymorphism is present, MTHFR enzyme activity
     is reduced.
  2. In particular, when the homozygous c.665C>T polymorphism (TT homozygote) is present,
     the association with hyperhomocysteinemia is higher than in the wild type, so
     measurement of blood homocysteine concentration is recommended. However, including
     MTHFR genotyping in the routine evaluation of thrombophilia or pregnancy loss is not
     recommended. (ref. 2013 ACMG Practice guideline)
     In addition, associations with the efficacy or adverse effects of fluoropyrimidine
     (5-FU) and antifolate (methotrexate) drug therapy have been reported, but the level
     of evidence has not been clearly established, and no genotype-based treatment
     guideline has been recommended.
  3. In Koreans, the frequencies of the c.665C>T genotype are reported as 35.8% for CC
     homozygous, 49.5% for CT heterozygous, and 14.7% for TT homozygous.
     (ref. J Korean Med Sci 2004; 19: 209-13)
  4. Note that genetic factors not identified by this assay and non-genetic factors such
     as drug-drug interactions may affect drug response. Therefore, comprehensive
     interpretation of the results and determination of the application policy are
     required.
