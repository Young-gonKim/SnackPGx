- COMMENTS
  1. CYP2C19 is an enzyme involved in the metabolism of drugs such as clopidogrel,
     voriconazole, proton pump inhibitors (e.g., omeprazole), diazepam, and phenytoin.
     Enzyme activity is determined by the CYP2C19 genotype.

  2. In patients with acute coronary syndrome treated with clopidogrel, those who are
     heterozygous (IM, intermediate metabolizer) or homozygous (PM, poor metabolizer)
     for variants associated with reduced CYP2C19 enzyme activity show lower active
     clopidogrel metabolite levels and an increased risk of adverse cardiovascular
     events compared with normal metabolizers carrying only wild-type alleles.
     Therefore, alternative antiplatelet therapy such as prasugrel or ticagrelor is
     recommended.
     (CPIC Guideline for clopidogrel and CYP2C19. Clin Pharmacol Ther 2022)

  3. During voriconazole therapy, patients who are heterozygous (IM, intermediate
     metabolizer) or homozygous (PM, poor metabolizer) for variants associated with
     reduced CYP2C19 enzyme activity may show higher blood drug concentrations relative
     to dose and an increased risk of adverse effects compared with normal metabolizers
     carrying only wild-type alleles. Therefore, careful dose adjustment together with
     therapeutic drug monitoring is required, and alternative drug therapy should be
     considered, particularly for PM.
     (CPIC Guideline for voriconazole and CYP2C19. Clin Pharmacol Ther 2022)
     Other influencing factors such as concomitant medications, hepatic function, and
     renal function should also be considered.

  4. The CYP2C19 *1, *2, *3, and *17 allele frequencies in Koreans are reported as
     approximately 60%, 28%, 10%, and 1.5%, respectively.
     (Ref. Journal of Clinical Pharmacy and Therapeutics (2010) 35, 697-703)

  5. The above results were prepared using the Gene-specific Information Tables from
     ClinPGx. (https://www.clinpgx.org/page/cyp2c19RefMaterials, downloaded 2025.10.20)

  6. Note that genetic factors not identified by this assay and non-genetic factors such
     as drug-drug interactions may affect drug response. Therefore, comprehensive
     interpretation of the results and determination of the application policy are
     required.
